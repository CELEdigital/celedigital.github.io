This document, written by Paula Vargas de Brea - CELE researcher - addresses the issue of the regulation of non-consensual pornography on the internet, with the aim of providing a description of the current law that allows for its application or to determine whether another type of regulation is needed to provide effective solutions that respect all human rights. According to some organisations and academic institutions, the vast majority of these types of actions affect women, and this is because the devaluation of women as women includes the exposure of their sexual preferences and attitudes. On the other hand, it analyses the remedies that the Civil and Commercial Code can provide and the most appropriate procedural route. The author argues that non-consensual pornography is of regulatory interest, as there is a right that has been violated, and the Argentine legal system should provide a mechanism for its redress. This paper approaches the issue from two perspectives that are not mutually exclusive. First, it approaches non-consensual pornography as a violation of a fundamental right. The author analyses whether it constitutes hate speech for inciting violence against women or whether it is content that violates the victim's privacy. Secondly, she addresses the phenomenon from a criminal perspective, analysing whether it is covered by an existing criminal offence and, if so, whether it is advisable to criminalise it.
