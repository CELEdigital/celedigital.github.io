This report presents a mapping of the main international, regional and national regulatory initiatives on artificial intelligence. In a scenario characterized by a growing normative competition to define regulatory models in the subject, most initiatives have points in common but---in many cases---differ in their approaches and their legal framing. We observe a consolidated trend towards regulatory frameworks focused on the risks involved in the development and implementation of technology, where the protection of human rights, although mentioned, is usually in the background. Even in the absence of a dominant regulatory model, it is likely that the first legally binding instruments that adopt this approach decisively influence future normative developments that take them as models, further displacing the focus on rights. Likewise, the limited participation of developing countries in these processes poses relevant challenges of inclusion and representativeness in the Global Governance of AI, with the risk of generating regulatory frameworks detached from local contexts and needs.
