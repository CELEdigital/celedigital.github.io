For more than a decade, various legislatures across Latin America have been enacting laws and proposing initiatives aimed at directly or indirectly regulating the digital space. These spaces are an essential part of the personal, political, and cultural lives of millions of people. Moreover, they serve as arenas where a wide range of human rights, including freedom of expression, are exercised and enjoyed. This evolving landscape raises a fundamental question: how does internet regulation impact the guarantees of freedom of expression in Latin America? In response to this issue, the Center for Studies on Freedom of Expression and Access to Information (CELE) at the University of Palermo established the Legislative Observatory on Freedom of Expression in Latin America. Its objective is to monitor legislative developments and analyze the impact of discussions surrounding the circulation of speech both online and offline at the regional level. This study outlines the methodology used and highlights key findings, trends, and legislative challenges in nine countries: Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, Guatemala, Mexico, Paraguay, and Peru. Through a quantitative and qualitative analysis of laws and legislative proposals, it aims to reflect on recurring topics in the legislative agenda, the criminalization of speech, the legality and proportionality of regulatory measures, trends in digital ecosystem regulation, and freedom of expression during the health emergency, among other issues. Finally, the study presents some shared conclusions regarding the regulation of freedom of expression in Latin America.
