In the relationship between the judiciary and the press there are a series of conventions and habits that have shaped a culture of legal and judicial information over the years. Often, this culture generates a series of tensions between both actors, which also affects public opinion. In this framework, the Center for Studies on Freedom of Expression and Access to Information (CELE) with the support of UNESCO present a Guide of Recommendations aimed at contributing to the construction of a bond of respect and collaboration between the press and the Judiciary that favors freedom of expression, access to information and, ultimately, human rights and democratic principles. From the Argentine experience, some of the conflicts that most broadly govern the relationship between the press and the Judiciary beyond borders are presented here, and a series of recommendations are identified regarding the processes of production and dissemination of legal information. The Guides are the product of the investigation of existing frameworks at the local and international level and of the reflection generated from the workshop held on May 20, 2016, at the headquarters of the University of Palermo in the City of Buenos Aires, with the participation of press professionals, judges and relevant actors of the Judiciary from various countries and legal cultures.
