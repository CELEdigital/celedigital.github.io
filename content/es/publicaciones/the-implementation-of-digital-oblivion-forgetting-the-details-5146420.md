Technological advancement and the expansion of the Internet have transformed the way information is stored, shared and accessed. However, this permanent access poses significant challenges in terms of privacy, reputation and freedom of expression. The “right to be forgotten” emerges as a tool to address these issues, especially when the published information negatively affects a person’s life, even if it is no longer relevant today.
