This study focuses on the application and potential misinterpretation of Law 5777/16 in Paraguay, which addresses comprehensive protection for women against all forms of violence, and the use of its precautionary measures in cases of public interest that may negatively impact freedom of expression. The first part of the research analyzes Paraguay’s National Constitution in relation to the protection of freedom of expression, as well as the international standards established by the Inter-American human rights system, including case law from the Inter-American Court of Human Rights (IACHR). This section places particular emphasis on prior censorship, the protection threshold applicable to public officials and public figures in the exercise of their freedom of expression, and how this may be compromised by misinterpretations of Law 5777/16 by national courts. Additionally, the study presents an analysis of six legal cases in Paraguay that illustrate the challenges and risks associated with the application of this law, highlighting the need for a balanced approach that safeguards both women's rights and freedom of expression, as well as due legal process in a democratic society.
